👋 Welcome to Settlement Suite™ Pro

This tool helps you track settlement negotiations from start to finish. You don't need to be a lawyer or mediator to use it — just follow the steps in each tab. Everything is automatic. You type in numbers and it does the math for you.

Getting Started — Do This First
1
Open the tool in Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge. These browsers support the live voice recording feature. Other browsers work but won't record audio.
2
Look at the top bar. You'll see the case name. The tool comes loaded with a sample case so you can see how everything looks.
3
The screen has three sections: Left = notes and recording. Middle = your main working tabs. Right = live dashboard that updates automatically.
4
Start the session timer by clicking the Start button in the top bar. This tracks how long your session has been running.
💡 Tip: Save this file to your computer. Open a new copy for each case so your notes stay separate.
Left Panel — Transcript & Notes
1
Choose a room first. Click Joint, Claimant, Respondent, or Break. This tells the tool which room you're in so your notes are labeled correctly.
2
To record live audio: Click the red Record button. Your browser will ask for microphone permission — click Allow. Everything said will appear as text automatically.
3
To type a note manually: Click the text box at the bottom of the left panel, type your note, and press Enter. It gets stamped with the time and room.
4
To save the transcript: Click the Save .txt button. A text file downloads to your computer with all your notes and timestamps.
5
To search your notes: Type a word in the search bar at the top of the left panel. It highlights every place that word appears.
💡 Tip: Switch rooms every time you move — click the room button before you start a caucus. This keeps your notes clean and organized.
Tab 1 — Offer Tracker
1
This is your main negotiation log. Every time one side makes a new demand or offer, you record it here.
2
CP demand = the amount the Claimant (the person who filed the case) is asking for.
3
Respondent offer = the amount the other side is offering to pay.
4
Type the dollar amounts and click the green Add button. The tool automatically calculates the gap (difference between the two numbers) and the midpoint (the number right in the middle).
5
Suggested next move — the tool suggests what each side might offer next to keep things moving. These are just ideas, not rules.
6
Watch the right dashboard — it updates automatically every time you add a round.
💡 Tip: Add a short note for each round (like "after first caucus") so you remember what changed and why.
Tab 2 — Bracketing Calculator
1
What is bracketing? A bracket is when a side gives a high and low number — like "we'll go somewhere between $60,000 and $85,000." It's a way to negotiate without committing to one number.
2
Enter the Claimant's high and low bracket numbers on the left side. Enter the Respondent's bracket numbers on the right.
3
The tool calculates: where the brackets overlap (if at all), the suggested split in the middle, and whether there's a Zone of Potential Agreement (ZOPA).
4
ZOPA = Zone of Potential Agreement. If the brackets overlap, both sides have shown they could agree somewhere in that range. This is a good sign!
💡 Tip: If brackets don't overlap, the gap shows how far apart they still are. Use the midpoint suggestion to propose a bridging number.
Tab 3 — Damages & Wages Calculator
1
Use this tab to figure out what a case might be worth based on lost wages, emotional distress, and other damages.
2
Lost wages: Enter the person's salary or hourly pay and how many weeks they were out of work. The tool calculates gross back pay automatically.
3
Offsets: Enter any unemployment benefits or other income earned during that time. These reduce the net amount owed.
4
Additional damages: Add amounts for emotional distress, future lost wages, retirement losses, or other economic claims on the right side.
5
Settlement range: The tool gives you a Low and High estimate based on the multipliers. This is the likely negotiating range for this case.
💡 Tip: This is for discussion only — not a legal calculation. Always confirm numbers with the attorney involved.
Tab 4 — Allocation (Attorney Fees & Tax Split)
1
What is allocation? When a settlement is paid, part of the money goes to the attorney as a fee. This tab shows how much the client actually takes home.
2
Enter the total settlement amount and the attorney's fee percentage (like 33% for a standard contingency fee).
3
W-2 vs. 1099 split: Settlement money is often split into two types for tax purposes. W-2 money is taxed like regular wages. 1099 money is treated differently. The tool shows both.
4
Net to claimant = the amount the client receives after the attorney takes their fee. This is the number that matters most to the person settling.
💡 Tip: Always tell people to consult a tax professional about W-2 vs. 1099 treatment. The split can affect how much they owe in taxes.
Tab 5 — Proposal Generator
1
Use this tab to create a written settlement proposal that you can print or copy and share with both sides.
2
Fill in the fields: settlement amount, W-2/1099 split, payment deadline, and key terms like confidentiality and neutral reference.
3
The proposal writes itself as you fill in the fields. It updates in real time in the green box below the inputs.
4
Click Print Proposal to print a clean version, or Copy Text to paste it into an email or document.
💡 Tip: This is a discussion proposal, not a legal agreement. Have the attorney draft the final settlement agreement and release.
Tab 6 — AI Summary
1
This tab generates a summary of your entire session based on everything logged in the transcript on the left.
2
Click "Generate full summary" to see a complete recap — current positions, key issues, caucus notes, and a suggested path forward.
3
You can also generate summaries by room: Click Joint, Claimant, or Respondent to see just that room's highlights.
4
Print the summary for your records or to share with the parties at the end of the session.
💡 Tip: The more notes you log in the left panel during the session, the more detailed and accurate the summary will be.
Right Panel — Live Dashboard
1
The right panel updates automatically every time you add an offer round. You don't have to do anything.
2
Gap closed = total amount the parties have moved toward each other since round 1. Watch this number grow as negotiation progresses.
3
Negotiation phase shows where you are in the process: Early Exploration → Active Negotiation → Narrowing → Settlement Range Reached.
4
Non-monetary terms at the bottom let you check off things like neutral reference, confidentiality, and reinstatement as they are agreed upon.
